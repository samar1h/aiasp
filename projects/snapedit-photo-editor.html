How Snapedit works

A quick tour of every tool, explained plainly.

Filters

One-tap looks (like black & white or a warm vintage tone) applied on top of your photo. Tap a thumbnail to preview it instantly; tap "Original" to remove it.

Adjust

Fine-tune brightness, contrast, color and sharpness with sliders. Drag left to reduce, right to increase. "Reset" puts every slider back to its starting point.

Crop

Drag the corners or edges of the box to choose what part of the photo to keep. Pick a ratio chip (like 1:1 for square) to snap to a fixed shape, or use Straighten to level a tilted horizon. Tap "Apply Crop" to confirm — nothing is cut until you do.

Rotate

Turn the photo 90° at a time, or flip it like a mirror horizontally or vertically.

Resize

Change the pixel dimensions of your photo — useful for shrinking a file before uploading somewhere. The lock icon keeps width and height in the same proportion so your photo doesn't stretch.

Export

Choose a file type — JPG for smaller everyday photos, PNG if you need transparency or zero quality loss, WEBP for a modern smaller file — then download the result to your device.

Undo / Redo

The two arrows at the top-left step backward and forward through your edit history, so you can always recover from a mistake.

Start over?

Opening a new photo will discard your current edits.
